B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering

The Department of Mechanical Engineering emphasizes thermodynamics, manufacturing processes, and robotics.

Introduction

The Mechanical Engineering (Mech) Department is one of the oldest and most versatile branches of engineering, focusing on the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems. It plays a crucial role in various industries and innovations. Key highlights include:

Core Areas of Focus:

  • Thermodynamics and Heat Transfer: Study of energy systems, engines, and HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning).
  • Design and Manufacturing: CAD (Computer-Aided Design), CAM (Computer-Aided Manufacturing), and advanced manufacturing techniques.
  • Fluid Mechanics: Analysis of fluid behavior in systems like turbines, pumps, and pipelines.
  • Material Science: Understanding materials used in mechanical systems to optimize performance and durability.
  • Robotics and Automation: Development of automated systems and robotics for industrial applications.

Practical Learning:

  • Hands-on training in workshops and laboratories.
  • Real-world projects in design, manufacturing, and energy systems.
  • Internships in industries like automotive, aerospace, and energy.

Interdisciplinary Opportunities:

  • Overlaps with aerospace, automotive, electronics, and computer science in fields like mechatronics, AI, and energy systems.
