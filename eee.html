B.Tech in Electrical and Electronics Engineering

The Department of EEE focuses on power systems, renewable energy, and electrical circuit analysis.

Introduction

The Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE) Department is a dynamic and versatile branch of engineering that combines the study of electrical systems and electronic technologies. It focuses on the generation, transmission, and utilization of electrical power, along with the design and application of electronic devices. Key aspects include: Core Areas of Focus: Electrical Engineering: Power systems, machines, energy generation (conventional and renewable), and electrical infrastructure. Electronics: Circuit design, microcontrollers, and control systems. Control Systems: Automation, robotics, and industrial control processes. Power Electronics: Converting and managing electrical energy using advanced electronic systems. Practical Learning: Extensive lab work involving power systems, circuits, and machinery. Hands-on projects and internships to solve real-world engineering problems. Interdisciplinary Opportunities: Integration with mechanical engineering, computer science, and renewable energy for diverse applications such as smart grids, electric vehicles, and IoT.
